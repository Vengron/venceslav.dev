Common Questions

Answers before the call.

Are you actually available on-site next week? +

Yes — I'm based in Southeast Asia and travel across the region regularly. Typical lead time for an on-site visit is 1–2 weeks from confirmed engagement. Remote kickoffs can start within days. The Discovery Sprint includes one on-site day within the 3-day scope.

We tried AI tools before and they didn't stick. Why would this be different? +

Most AI tool deployments fail because they're imposed on a team without building understanding. Every engagement here is built around transferring the thinking, not just the tool. Your team will understand why the system works — and be able to fix it, extend it, or replace it when needed. That's what "skills, not dependencies" means in practice.

Our production data is messy — will that be a problem? +

It's the norm. The Discovery Sprint includes a data readiness assessment — we'll identify what you have, what you need, and what's actually required for each use case. Often the most valuable early output is a clear data collection strategy, not an AI model. We don't start building until the data foundation is sound.

What happens after the engagement ends? +

Every engagement ends with full documentation, implementation guides, and 30 days of async support (email/WhatsApp). The goal is always that your team owns the result and can continue without me. For clients who want ongoing support, quarterly retainers start at €12,000 (3 months, 4 hours/week, 2 on-site visits included).
